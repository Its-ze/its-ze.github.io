Creating Blog Content: My Workflow

Writing blog content has become an essential part of sharing my journey in the IT field, documenting my projects, and helping others learn from my experiences. I've developed a workflow that helps me produce high-quality blog posts without feeling overwhelmed. Here's how I create my blog content, from idea to published article.

Voice Recording and Transcription

The first step in my workflow often involves voice recording my thoughts. I use an old Sony audio recorder to capture my ideas, which lets me focus on getting my thoughts out without worrying about typing them up. This is especially useful for days when I have a lot of ideas but not enough time to sit and type. Once I have my recordings, I use transcription software to convert them into text. This raw text serves as the foundation of my blog post.

Using ChatGPT for Structuring and Editing

After I have my transcribed text, I turn to ChatGPT for help with structuring and editing. I provide it with the raw content and guide it on how I want the blog post to be organized. This process allows me to transform my thoughts into a more coherent and polished structure.

One of the key benefits of using ChatGPT is its ability to maintain my voice and style while improving clarity. I still retain full control over the content—I review every suggestion and make changes where needed to ensure the final product aligns with my vision. ChatGPT also helps me create titles, subtitles, and improve the flow of the text, which makes the blog more engaging and easier to read.

Final Proofreading and Personal Touches

Once the content is structured, I go through a final proofreading stage. This step is crucial because it allows me to add my personal touch and make sure everything reads smoothly. I often add anecdotes or insights that come to mind during this review, which adds depth to the content and helps it resonate more with readers.

Tools I Use: Sony Recorder, ChatGPT, and Personal Editing

My workflow relies on a combination of traditional and modern tools. The Sony audio recorder helps me capture thoughts without interruption, ChatGPT assists in organizing and polishing those thoughts, and my personal editing ensures that the final product is both authentic and high-quality.

Using ChatGPT During College

I also used a similar approach to create essays during college. I provided ChatGPT with the teacher’s rulebook and had it ask detailed questions to gather the information needed for the essay. I ensured that ChatGPT did not generate any content on its own but instead used the answers I provided. I would then review the generated essay in detail, making adjustments and ensuring it met my needs, adding my own parts and final touches. This ensured that the final piece was entirely my own work, just structured and optimized by ChatGPT.

Conclusion

Creating blog content doesn’t have to be stressful or time-consuming. By leveraging technology like ChatGPT and pairing it with traditional tools like voice recorders, I’ve developed a workflow that works well for me. This approach allows me to share my knowledge in an organized and accessible way while ensuring that each post stays true to my own voice and experiences.
